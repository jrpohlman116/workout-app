# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Serious competitive powerlifters training for meets. They follow structured wave periodization (Juggernaut method), track squat/bench/deadlift maxes, care about Wilks and DOTS scores, and plan around meet dates. They use this app mid-session, chalk on their hands, often on a phone in a loud gym. Precision matters more than aesthetics. They'll notice if the numbers are wrong.

## Product Purpose

A powerlifting training tool that manages Juggernaut-method wave cycles, calculates working weights from training maxes, tracks 1RM progression, and orients athletes in their meet-prep timeline. Success looks like an athlete who opens the app, knows exactly what to lift and why, logs it, and closes it. The app is the coach's playbook, not the coach.

## Brand Personality

Raw, athletic, no-nonsense. The app feels like a serious training partner who does the math without making it a performance. Confidence without posturing. Data without decoration. References: Strava segments, Garmin Connect — athletic data tools that respect the user's intelligence and get out of the way.

## Anti-references

- **Bro-culture supplement brands**: neon on black, aggressive display fonts, overly masculine energy, flames and lightning bolts. Avoid the aesthetic entirely.
- **Consumer wellness apps** (MyFitnessPal, Nike Training): bright pastels, rounded cartoon icons, motivational filler copy, calorie-tracking vibes.
- **SaaS dashboard clichés**: blue-indigo gradients, hero metric card grids, stock-photo-energy illustrations.
- **Gamified engagement loops** (Fitbit, Duolingo): streaks, badges, confetti, dopamine-trap patterns.

## Design Principles

1. **The tool disappears.** Every element on screen serves a training decision. If it doesn't help the athlete know what to lift, when, or how much, it doesn't belong.
2. **Data earns its place.** Numbers are shown because they're needed, not because they look impressive. Wilks scores, projected maxes, and wave labels must be immediately readable at a glance from arm's length.
3. **Earned confidence.** Tone is direct and assured, never hyped. The app has done the math; it doesn't need to celebrate itself.
4. **Athletic utility.** Designed for chalk-dusted hands on a phone mid-session. Touch targets are generous, states are unambiguous, and nothing requires careful reading.
5. **Precision over flair.** Correctness first, aesthetics second. Typography and spacing exist to make information scannable, not to make the screen attractive.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA strict. All interactive elements meet contrast minimums and have accessible names. Reduced motion support is already partially in place (ReducedMotionWrapper component) and should be maintained. Touch targets minimum 44x44px.
